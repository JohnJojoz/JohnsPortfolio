The Design Process

Content Audit

Previous State

During my internship at a startup, I conducted a content audit for the company's outdated website. The site lacked modern design elements and failed to clearly communicate the core benefits of Paperplanes, a programmatic direct mail service. The landing page, in particular, didn't effectively inform visitors about the business, and users had to scroll down to get key information. Additionally, the design lacked consistency across pages, with some sections missing a clear hierarchy of information. While the site had a valuable educational platform featuring blogs, white papers, and articles, it wasn’t fully optimized to engage visitors or convert them into leads.

New State

The content audit led to a number of improvements in the website redesign. On the landing page, I recommended integrating key phrases like "Eliminate waste. Decrease spend. Convert More" into a prominent slideshow, ensuring visitors grasped Paperplanes’ value immediately. To enhance user engagement, I proposed placing a video on the homepage featuring the team explaining what the business does, adding authenticity. I also emphasized the need for more cohesive design language and restructured several pages to improve the hierarchy of information. Importantly, all CTAs were redirected to a central contact form, ensuring smooth lead capture and conversion.

Laying out the Content

In the redesigned website, the Features Section was structured to highlight Paperplanes’ unique benefits using a visually appealing blend of text and images. This section emphasized the key advantages of programmatic direct mail, such as cost savings, enhanced personalization, and superior targeting capabilities, making the benefits clear to potential clients. To build credibility, I incorporated a Client Testimonials and Case Studies section, showcasing real-world results and the tangible benefits businesses have gained from using Paperplanes. This helped foster trust and persuade visitors to explore the service further. Additionally, I created a Educational Content and Resources section, dedicated to organizing blogs, white papers, and articles in a clean, accessible way, allowing users to easily educate themselves on programmatic direct mail. Finally, the redesigned site featured a persistent Call-to-Action (CTA), with clear pathways leading users to a centralized contact form, simplifying the conversion process and encouraging visitors to engage
